# Strategic Assessment: NYC Council Elevator Advocacy Coalition

## Overview
This document maps the strategic alignment, legislative history, and coalition potential for the key NYC Councilmembers identified as high-priority partners for the Elevator Advocacy Platform.

---

## 1. The "Heavy Hitters" (Committee Chairs)

### **Chris Banks (District 42, Brooklyn)**
*   **Role:** Chair, **Committee on Public Housing**.
*   **Focus:** NYCHA accountability, "Fair Share" shelter parity, senior services.
*   **Strategic Fit:** A former tenant organizer with a history of suing the city for equity. Our data on **7 Hegeman Ave** (11 recent complaints) is a direct "hook" for his committee's oversight work.

### **Crystal Hudson (District 35, Brooklyn)**
*   **Role:** Chair, **Committee on General Welfare**.
*   **Focus:** "Aging in Place," Black Agenda for NYC, NYCHA funding.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Her focus on seniors is perfectly aligned with our "Martha-First" UX. She has already introduced legislation to notify tenants of building violations proactively.

### **Carmen De La Rosa (District 10, Manhattan)**
*   **Role:** Member, Progressive & Women’s Caucuses.
*   **Focus:** Rent stabilization, "Right to Counsel," tenant protections.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Represents the district with the most rent-stabilized units in NYC. As a co-author of the 2019 State Housing Act, she understands the legal necessity of documented "Loss of Service."

---

## 2. The "Local Champions" (Regional Partners)

### **Justin Sanchez (District 17, Bronx)**
*   **Role:** Freshman Representative (Karl's Councilmember).
*   **Focus:** Quality-of-life, sanitation, LGBTQIA+ advocacy.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Eager for "early wins." His "Clean the Damn Streets" platform translates well into building-level maintenance and elevator reliability.

### **Althea Stevens (District 16, Bronx)**
*   **Focus:** Tenant education, CASA partnership, youth services.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Hosts an annual Housing Conference. She is the bridge to the South Bronx tenant union movement and a key regional ally for Justin Sanchez.

### **Amanda Farías (District 18, Bronx)**
*   **Role:** Council Majority Leader.
*   **Focus:** Transparency, Land Use, Co-op reform.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Her focus on enforceable timelines and board transparency suggests she will support the public disclosure of "hidden" elevator failure data.

---

## 3. Coalition Potential
- **The "Bronx Power Block":** Sanchez, Stevens, and Farías.
- **The "Brooklyn Power Block":** Banks and Hudson.
- **The "Manhattan Anchor":** De La Rosa.

**Synthesis:** By presenting this coalition with unified **Loss of Service** data across their districts, the Advocacy Platform transitions from a local building tool into a city-wide legislative standard for housing accountability.

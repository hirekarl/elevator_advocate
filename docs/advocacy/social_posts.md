## LinkedIn

**Angle:** Civic tech builder introducing the project to peers, funders, and potential partners.

---

When a building's elevator goes down in the Bronx, a senior on a walker doesn't just miss an errand. She's trapped. For hours. Sometimes overnight.

That's not a maintenance problem. It's a public health crisis — and it happens thousands of times a year in NYC, with almost no accountability.

I'm a fellow at Pursuit's AI-Native program, and I've spent the last several months building a response to that: **Elevator Advocate**, a free tool that puts the city's own elevator complaint data directly in the hands of tenants and the council members who represent them.

Here's what it does:
- **Verified outage records.** Two neighbors report the same outage within two hours → the system marks it verified. Management can't dismiss it. It holds up in Housing Court.
- **One-tap advocacy.** Any tenant can generate a 311 script or a council office email tailored to their building's specific complaint history.
- **Family alerts.** When the elevator goes down, the people who need to know — a daughter in Queens, a neighbor upstairs — are notified immediately.

The seasonal urgency is real: NYC elevator complaints spike **33% every July**, a pattern that holds across every year of city data going back to 2018. The buildings most at risk are already racking up complaints.

We're in MVP now, targeting six priority council districts in the Bronx and Brooklyn. If you work in civic tech, housing advocacy, or tenant organizing — or if you know someone who does — I'd love to connect.

🔗 [elevatoradvocate.nyc](https://www.elevatoradvocate.nyc)

# Community Organization Research — South Bronx Outreach

**Last updated:** April 24, 2026
**Context:** Potential partners for Elevator Advocate tenant outreach in District 17 and the broader South Bronx. All orgs are in or adjacent to D17 (Port Morris, Mott Haven, Hunts Point, Longwood).

---

## CASA (Community Action for Safe Apartments)

**Address:** 35 Marcy Place, Bronx, NY 10452
**Focus:** Tenant organizing in rent-stabilized buildings, Southwest Bronx. 3,000+ members. Fiscal host: New Settlement.
**Founded:** 2005

**Current priorities:**
- Right to Counsel expansion (flagship) — Bronx representation rates collapsed from 81% (Q4 2021) to 31% (Q4 2024)
- Anti-MCI (Major Capital Improvement) advocacy — opposing rent increases tied to building upgrades
- Rent Justice Coalition — rent freezes on rent-stabilized leases

**Key contacts:**
- **Hal Bergold** — Lead Organizer
- **Pablo Estupinan** — Lead Tenant Organizer / Deputy Director | p.estupinan@newsettlement.org

**Fit for Elevator Advocate:** Mixed-strong. Litigation-focused. Best entry point: frame elevator documentation as **evidence for housing court cases** — timestamped outage history strengthens Right to Counsel cases and counters landlord claims. "Better data for court" is a hook they'll recognize.

**Relationship to D17:** Active in Sanchez's district. Possible warm intro through Kevin Collado's office.

## Mothers on the Move (MOM / Madres en Movimiento)

**Website:** mothersonthemove.org | @Momsonthemove1
**Focus:** Member-led organizing by low-income women of color. Longwood, Mott Haven, Hunts Point, Port Morris. Demographics: Latinx (Puerto Rican, Honduran, Mexican, Dominican), Black, West African.
**Founded:** 1992

**Current priorities:**
- Tenant association formation; community-controlled housing redevelopment
- Environmental justice (South Bronx Community Resiliency Agenda coalition)
- Building safety, air quality, heat — habitability as a climate resilience issue

**Key contacts:**
- **Wanda Salaman** — Executive Director (at MOM since 2002)

**Fit for Elevator Advocate:** Strong. MOM's EJ framing explicitly covers building habitability and heat/safety — elevator access for seniors, families with strollers, and disabled residents is a natural extension. They've partnered across organizations and are open to tools that strengthen member organizing. Frame as a **habitability data tool** that feeds into their community safety work. Summer heat + broken elevator = life-threatening isolation for elderly residents lands immediately in their EJ lens.

**Relationship to D17:** Active in Sanchez's district. Possible warm intro through Kevin Collado's office.

## South Bronx Tenant Movement (SBTM)

**Location:** Mott Haven-based; organized through South Bronx Tomorrow
**Focus:** Grassroots tenant association organizing in privately-owned buildings across the South Bronx.
**Key contact:** **Manny Pardilla** (organizer/coordinator)

**Current work:** Already uses strategic 311 complaint filing as core organizing tactic — one campaign filed ~100 violations in an 18-unit building using coordinated photo documentation. Works with TakeRoot Justice for legal support.

**Fit for Elevator Advocate:** Highest direct alignment of any org found. SBTM is already using 311 as organizing infrastructure and documenting failures with no dedicated tech platform. Verified elevator outage records and 311 script generation would slot directly into their existing workflow.

## TakeRoot Justice (Housing Division)

**Website:** takerootjustice.org
**Focus:** Legal, participatory research, and policy support for South Bronx tenant organizing (Mott Haven, Districts 14/15). Partners with SBTM.
**Key contacts:** Michael Grinthal (Director of Housing); Keriann Pauls (Interim Executive Director)

**Current work:** Documents "immediately hazardous" building conditions (mold, leaks, no gas) for housing court litigation. Won a $70,000+ class action settlement (2021) for tenants in a building with 7 months of no cooking gas.

**Fit for Elevator Advocate:** Uses HPD violations database and Housing Court records — no proprietary data platform. Would benefit from timestamped, resident-verified outage records as litigation-grade evidence. Stronger fit than CASA on building conditions specifically; more specialized.

## Banana Kelly Community Improvement Association

**Website:** bkcianyc.org
**Location:** Longwood / Hunts Point; founded 1978
**Focus:** Housing development and community organizing. Operates 55 multifamily buildings (1,350 units) across Longwood, Hunts Point, Mott Haven, Morrisania.
**Key contacts:** Hope Burgess (CEO); Brian Sahd (Director of Resource Development)

**Current work:** In 2020, organized tenants to file ~300 HPD complaints for buildings at 741–743 Hunts Point Ave and 1202 Spofford Ave. Runs tenant associations, rental assistance, ESL services.

**Fit for Elevator Advocate:** Banana Kelly owns and manages buildings — they have fiduciary interest in habitability, not just advocacy. Elevator data would help them prioritize internal repairs and benchmark against external stock. Different use case: operator accountability, not pure tenant organizing.

## Heat Seek

**Website:** heatseek.org
**Focus:** Tech nonprofit deploying IoT temperature sensors to document heating violations. Partners with 50–75 households in active tenant struggles during heat season (Oct–May).
**Key work:** Sensors create mesh network collecting hourly temperature data; tenants use it to galvanize rent strikes, defend evictions, and negotiate settlements in housing court. Already partners with **CASA** on South Bronx deployments.

**Fit for Elevator Advocate:** The closest organizational model to what Elevator Advocate does — tech-enabled data collection on building failures, integrated with tenant organizing. Heat Seek is seasonal (heat only, Oct–May); Elevator Advocate fills the year-round vertical mobility gap. **Potential peer partnership** rather than just a community partner — their tenant network and ours could be complementary without overlap.

**Note:** A January 2025 CITY article connected the Parkash family (owner of 89-20 161 Street — D27's top chronic offender) to an HPD heat sensor enforcement case involving a Bronx fire. Worth tracking.

## Northwest Bronx Community and Clergy Coalition (NWBCCC)

**Website:** northwestbronx.org
**Focus:** Tenant association development, heat/repair organizing, housing preservation. Northwest and Central Bronx (overlaps Districts 17–18 Tremont area). Founded 1970s.

**Current work:** Bronx Healthy Buildings Project, Bronx Community Land Trust, Tenants Demand Repairs campaign. Involved in HPD Heat Sensor Program. Featured in NYC Comptroller's 2025 "Turn Up the Heat" audit (Northwest Bronx led the city in heat complaints, 2023).

**Fit for Elevator Advocate:** Health-equity framing (building conditions as social determinants) overlaps with disability access. Elevator reliability is a health equity issue for elderly and disabled residents. Less elevator-specific than others, but experienced with IoT + tenant data and habitability documentation.

## Peer Tech Platform: JustFix.nyc

**Website:** justfix.org
**What they are:** An existing NYC tech platform for tenant organizing — tools include "Who Owns What" (portfolio research), letter-of-complaint generators, and HPD violation tracking. Not a community org; a civic tech peer.

**Relevance:** JustFix is the closest existing tech analogue to Elevator Advocate citywide. Understanding how they've positioned and partnered informs how Elevator Advocate should differentiate (real-time verification, elevator-specific, 2-hour consensus window). Not a partner candidate — a reference point.

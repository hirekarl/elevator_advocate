# Strategic Assessment: NYC Council Elevator Advocacy Coalition

## Overview
This document maps the strategic alignment, legislative history, and coalition potential for the key NYC Councilmembers identified as high-priority partners for the Elevator Advocacy Platform.

---

## 1. The "Heavy Hitters" (Committee Chairs)

### **Pierina Ana Sanchez (District 14, Bronx)**
*   **Role:** Chair, **Committee on Housing and Buildings**.
*   **Focus:** Tenant protections, building code enforcement, housing accountability.
*   **Strategic Fit:** The highest-leverage single contact in the NYC Council for this work. As Housing & Buildings Committee Chair, she sets the agenda for landlord accountability legislation. D14 (University Heights, Fordham, Belmont) has its own elevator complaint burden — offering to run a D14 analysis is the natural entry point. The D17 pilot is proof of concept; she is the audience who can make it legislation.
*   **Status:** Not yet contacted. Draft at `docs/advocacy/outreach/councilmember_pierina_sanchez_outreach.md`. Prerequisite: run `generate_district_reports --district 14` first.

### **Chris Banks (District 42, Brooklyn)**
*   **Role:** Chair, **Committee on Public Housing**.
*   **Focus:** NYCHA accountability, "Fair Share" shelter parity, senior services.
*   **Strategic Fit:** A former tenant organizer with a history of suing the city for equity. Our data on **7 Hegeman Ave** (11 recent complaints) is a direct "hook" for his committee's oversight work.

### **Crystal Hudson (District 35, Brooklyn)**
*   **Role:** Chair, **Committee on General Welfare**.
*   **Focus:** "Aging in Place," Black Agenda for NYC, NYCHA funding.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Her focus on seniors is perfectly aligned with our "Martha-First" UX. She has already introduced legislation to notify tenants of building violations proactively.

### **Carmen De La Rosa (District 10, Manhattan)**
*   **Role:** Member, Progressive & Women’s Caucuses.
*   **Focus:** Rent stabilization, "Right to Counsel," tenant protections.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Represents the district with the most rent-stabilized units in NYC. As a co-author of the 2019 State Housing Act, she understands the legal necessity of documented "Loss of Service."

## 2. The "Local Champions" (Regional Partners)

### **Justin Sanchez (District 17, Bronx)**
*   **Role:** Freshman Representative (Karl's Councilmember).
*   **Focus:** Quality-of-life, sanitation, LGBTQIA+ advocacy.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Eager for "early wins." His "Clean the Damn Streets" platform translates well into building-level maintenance and elevator reliability.

### **Althea Stevens (District 16, Bronx)**
*   **Focus:** Tenant education, CASA partnership, youth services.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Hosts an annual Housing Conference. She is the bridge to the South Bronx tenant union movement and a key regional ally for Justin Sanchez.

### **Amanda Farías (District 18, Bronx)**
*   **Role:** Council Majority Leader.
*   **Focus:** Transparency, Land Use, Co-op reform.
*   **Strategic Fit:** Her focus on enforceable timelines and board transparency suggests she will support the public disclosure of "hidden" elevator failure data.

### **Shekar Krishnan (District 26, Queens)**
*   **Role:** NYC Council Member; Housing Committee member.
*   **Focus:** Tenant protections, ADA accessibility, immigrant communities.
*   **Strategic Fit:** On approximately April 21, 2026, Krishnan co-led a demonstration at 7 train stations demanding MTA elevator upgrades. He has publicly called inaccessible transit "unacceptable." His district (Jackson Heights, Corona, Elmhurst) covers a dense, multi-lingual renter population with its own elevator reliability burden in residential buildings. The Pursuit connection (LIC, Queens) is a potential warm introduction vector.
*   **Key framing rule:** The MTA demonstration is the entry point only — do NOT conflate subway station ADA elevators with residential building elevators. The residential angle is the natural complement, not the same fight.
*   **Status:** Not yet contacted. Draft at `docs/advocacy/outreach/councilmember_krishnan_outreach.md`. Prerequisite: run `generate_district_reports --district 26` first.

## 3. Coalition Potential
- **The "Bronx Power Block":** Sanchez (D17), Stevens (D16), and Farías (D18).
- **The "Brooklyn Power Block":** Banks (D42) and Hudson (D35).
- **The "Manhattan Anchor":** De La Rosa (D10).
- **The "Queens Moment":** Krishnan (D26) — activated by MTA demonstration; residential angle is the bridge.
- **The Committee Chair Priority:** Pierina Ana Sanchez (D14) — Housing & Buildings Chair; single highest-leverage contact.

**Synthesis:** By presenting this coalition with unified **Loss of Service** data across their districts, the Advocacy Platform transitions from a local building tool into a city-wide legislative standard for housing accountability. Pierina Ana Sanchez is the committee-level anchor; the Bronx Power Block creates cross-district political cover; Krishnan opens a Queens flank tied to a live political moment.

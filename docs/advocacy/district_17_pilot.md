# Proposal: Elevator Advocate — District 17 Pilot (Bronx)

## Overview
A strategic pilot program to deploy Elevator Advocate in NYC Council District 17 (Rep. Justin Sanchez), focusing on high-density residential corridors in Port Morris, Mott Haven, and Melrose.

## Strategic Partners (Institutional Sponsorship)

### 1. Community Action for Safe Apartments (CASA) / New Settlement
- **Role:** Primary organizing partner for building-wide tenant unions.
- **Value Prop:** Provides "Loss of Service" data for HP Actions (repair lawsuits).
- **Contact:** 1512 Townsend Ave, Bronx, NY.

### 2. Mothers on the Move (MOM) / Madres en Movimiento
- **Role:** Local grassroots partner for District 17 (Intervale Ave).
- **Value Prop:** Focuses on accessibility justice for seniors and disabled tenants.

### 3. Mobilization for Justice (MFJ)
- **Role:** Legal support partner for the "Campaign for Better Bronx Housing."
- **Value Prop:** Uses our quantified downtime reports as evidence in Housing Court.

## Targeted Impact
- **Metric:** Identify buildings with >10% Loss of Service in the South Bronx.
- **Advocacy Tool:** Automatic generation of 311 scripts and "Email Representative" drafts for Justin Sanchez.

## Pilot Building Selection Criteria
- High volume of SODA categories `'6S'` (elevator complaint) and `'6M'` (elevator/escalator).
- NYCHA or rent-stabilized status.
- Geocoded within District 17 boundaries.

## Multi-District Coalition Strategy
For a successful pilot, **Justin Sanchez (District 17)** should collaborate with the following representatives whose districts contain the highest-frequency elevator failures:
- **Althea Stevens (D16):** Adjacent to D17; high overlap in South Bronx tenant needs.
- **Chris Banks (D42):** Represents the highest-frequency hotspot (Brownsville).
- **Carmen De La Rosa (D10):** Key ally for Upper Manhattan tenant advocacy.
- **Crystal Hudson (D35) & Amanda Farías (D18):** Leaders in the Committee on Public Housing and Economic Development.

By forming this coalition, the Advocacy Platform can transition from a local tool to a city-wide standard for housing accountability.
